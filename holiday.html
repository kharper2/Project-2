The Best Holiday: Debate Settled

*Disclaimers*

  1. I will generally be looking at the major U.S. holidays that I like.
  2. Yes, I know this is biased, so please use your own judgment when it comes to my findings.
  3. If your judgement doesn't align with mine, I strongly suggest you to reconsider.

The suspects

  • New Year's Day
  • MLK Jr. Day
  • President's Day
  • Easter Sunday
  • Mother's Day
  • Memorial Day
  • Juneteenth
  • Father's Day
  • Independence Day
  • Labor Day
  • Indigenous People's Day
  • Veterans Day
  • Thanksgiving
  • Christmas

The first cut

Objectively, the best holidays are the ones that result with the most time off of school/work.

Summer doesn't count, it's time off. As a result, we eliminate Independence Day as it makes no difference in time off.

This leaves us with:

  • New Year's Day
  • Easter Sunday
  • Thanksgiving
  • Christmas

The second cut

I love fall and winter, so Easter is eliminated.

There is so much hope and work that goes into planning for Christmas that once the day passes, I go into a mild seasonal depression. Goodbye New Years.

The reveal

Although Christmas has familial and religious importance, it is a holiday that many companies profit off of.

Don't get me wrong, I love buying and receiving presents, but I believe that there is too much emphasis put on the materialistic side of this holiday.

Therefore, because of the emphasis on family (or friends if Friendsgiving is your thing) the winner is Thanksgiving!

Also the food is scrumptious.

And most importantly, this year, it falls on my 18th birthday :)
